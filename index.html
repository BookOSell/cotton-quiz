About The MCB Debate

The Manik Chandra Barooah Memorial National Level Debate Competition honours the visionary whose leadership led to the founding of Cotton College in 1901 — the institution that later became Cotton University.

First organized in 1979, the MCB Debate is held annually on the death anniversary of Manik Chandra Barooah as a tribute to his immense contribution to education and society in Assam. Today, it stands as a prestigious national-level competition drawing participants from the finest universities across India — including the University of Delhi, Jawaharlal Nehru University, Pondicherry University, and institutions from South-East Asia.

With over 50+ teams engaging in robust intellectual discourse, the MCB Debate has established itself as a glorious benchmark for debate competitions in North-East India.
